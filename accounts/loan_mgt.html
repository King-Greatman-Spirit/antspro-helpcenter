ANTSPRO II (HR) Loan Management Help Center

The HR environment of ANTSPRO II is designed to streamline employee management processes, providing tools for tracking staff information, leave approvals, and department-specific details. This guide will help users navigate the HR dashboard effectively.

Navigating the HR Dashboard

Dashboard Features

  1. Top Navigation Bar:
    • Includes tabs such as Accounts , My Profile , Leave , Password , Back and Refresh.
    • Use these options to view accounts related information, update personal information, manage leave requests, reset passwords, go back or refresh the page.
  2. Second Navigation Bar:
    • This navigation bar contains additional options designed to provide quick access to essential HR and financial services:
      • Pay Slips – View and download your monthly or annual pay slips. Access secure, detailed records for financial documentation and verification.
      • Loans – Manage personal or company-backed loan information. View balances, repayment schedules, and submit loan adjustment requests.
      • Loan Apps. – Apply for new loans directly from the dashboard. Track application status and review documentation requirements.
      • Help – Access support resources, FAQs, and troubleshooting guides for resolving technical issues and understanding dashboard functionalities.
      • Support – Contact HR or IT support for assistance. Submit tickets, access live chat, and track the status of support requests.
    • These options are designed to enhance user experience by offering direct access to payroll information, loan applications, loan management, personal account settings, and support services, all from a single navigation bar.

Employee Accounts: Loans Management

The Employee Accounts: Loans Management interface allows employees to manage their loans and advances directly through the AntsPro Dashboard. Below is a breakdown of each section for better understanding:

Create a New Deduction Profile

This section allows you to create a new deduction profile for an employee's loan management. Below is a description of each field to help you fill it out accurately:

  • Date: The date the deduction profile is being created. It automatically captures the current date, but it can be modified if necessary.
  • Type: The type of deduction being created. In this case, it is set to "Loans" to specify it is for loan management.
  • Code: A unique identifier for the loan type. For instance, '1032' represents the specific loan category.
  • Basic Salary: This field captures the employee's basic monthly salary. It is used to determine affordability for loan repayments.
  • Loan Amount: The total sum of money being borrowed by the employee. It should be entered without commas (e.g., '100000.00').
  • Repay Duration: The number of months over which the loan will be repaid. For example, '24' indicates the loan will be repaid in 24 monthly installments.
  • Repayment Starts: The date when loan repayments are scheduled to begin. This should be coordinated with payroll cycles.
  • Deductions Ends: The calculated date when all loan repayments will be completed, based on the duration and monthly deductions.
  • Monthly Deduction: The fixed amount that will be deducted from the employee's salary each month. It should also be entered without commas (e.g., '10000.00').
  • Remarks: Any additional comments or notes related to the loan profile, such as the purpose of the loan or specific repayment terms.
  • Approved By: The individual or department head who authorizes the loan deduction profile. This provides a record of authorization.
