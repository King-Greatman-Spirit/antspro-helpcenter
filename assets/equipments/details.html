Asset / Equipment Details Help Center

The Asset/Equipment Details section enables users to track, manage, and review company resources such as accounts vouchers and other field assets. Each record offers descriptive and tracking metadata for easier audit and verification.

Navigating and Using the Asset/Equipment Details Panel

The Asset/Equipment Details panel displays vital information including the category, description, serials, and historical metadata of a listed asset.

  1. Category: Type of record (e.g., Accounts Vouchers).
  2. Description: Specifies the asset use, e.g., Field Expenses – Hired Hands.
  3. ID No.: Unique tracking code for the asset, e.g., BGT/ADM/FEH001.
  4. Serial No. / Reg. No. / Chassis / Engine / Manufacturer / Model / Make / Colour: These fields appear as NA (Not Applicable), which means they are not required for this type of entry.
  5. Year of Manufacture: Indicates the year the expense was recorded (e.g., 2022-01-10).
  6. Asset Type: Classification under organizational tracking (e.g., Field Expense Voucher Codes).
  7. Date of Purchase: Indicates when this transaction or voucher was recorded.
  8. Depreciation: Percentage depreciation assigned to this expense item.
  9. Remark: Additional label used internally, such as "Project Field Expense Voucher".
  10. Log: Editable log area for entry notes or updates.

Supporting Documents & Metadata Tabs

The following document tabs provide quick access to detailed certification, maintenance, tracking, and technical information related to the asset or voucher.

  1. QTA/QAQC Cert: Quality Assurance and Quality Control certificates related to the asset.
  2. STEM: Science, Technology, Engineering, and Math-related documentation including technical justifications.
  3. GMRIS: General Material/Resource Inspection Sheet - A comprehensive specification report that includes:
    • Detailed material specifications and compliance standards
    • Inspection results with pass/fail status
    • Technical evaluations from quality teams
    • View/Print Functionality:
      • Each GMRIS record includes a ✔ View/Print button in the last column
      • Click to open the full inspection report in printable format
      • Allows downloading or printing for audit purposes
      • Maintains original formatting and signatures
  4. MTCE: Maintenance records tracking repairs, servicing schedules, and maintenance history.
  5. SRF: Service Request Form - The standardized workflow system for service requests featuring:
    • End-to-end tracking of service requests from submission to completion
    • Standard columns including:
      • Date of request
      • Unique SR No. (Service Request Number)
      • Detailed project description
      • Requestor information (Reg. By)
    • Approval Workflow:
      • Authorization status shows if request was reviewed by appropriate personnel
      • Approval column confirms final sign-off
      • Color-coded or icon-based status indicators (Approved/Rejected/Pending)
    • View/Action Controls:
      • ✓ View option for accessing full request details
      • Action buttons for authorized users to Approve/Reject
      • Audit trail showing all workflow steps and timestamps
    • Used for diverse operations including:
      • Equipment maintenance tracking
      • Budget allocation requests
      • Field operation approvals
      • Routine service scheduling
  6. Datasheets: Technical specification documents with manufacturer details.
  7. Tracking History: Complete chronological record of asset movements and status changes.

These documents are critical for technical evaluation, procurement tracking, and compliance audits.

Understanding the Tracking Table Section

Below the asset details, the tracking table logs the movement or issuance of the asset, which can be filtered and searched:

  1. Issue Date: Date the item was issued to a department or personnel.
  2. Return Date: Expected or completed date of return.
  3. From / To: Who issued it and who received it respectively.
  4. Issue Ref / Rec. Ref: Document references for validation.
  5. Search Bar: Quickly locate any log entry using keywords.
  6. Records Per Page: Lets you set the number of rows shown at a time.
  7. Pagination: Used to navigate between multiple pages of tracking records.
  8. No Data Available: If no tracking history exists yet, a "No data available" message is displayed.

Pro Tip: Always update remarks and logs to maintain complete audit trails for each record.

Accuracy is critical — double-check entries for compliance and accountability!
