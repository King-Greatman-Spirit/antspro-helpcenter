Cash Retirement Form Help Center

This comprehensive guide provides everything you need to successfully prepare, submit, and track Cash Retirement Forms (CRFs) for B.G. Technical Limited's Pipeline and Well Services operations.

About Cash Retirements

Cash Retirement Forms are mandatory documents that:

  • Provide accountability for cash advances received
  • Document actual expenses with supporting receipts
  • Reconcile any balance (excess or shortfall)
  • Create audit trails for all project expenditures

Forms must be submitted within 7 working days after advance utilization.

Getting Started

Who Should Use This?

  • Project team members who received cash advances
  • Department heads approving expenditures
  • Finance personnel processing retirements
  • Auditors verifying expense documentation

When to Submit?

  • Within 7 days of completing expenditures
  • Before project closeout for final accounting
  • When requested during audit processes
  • Prior to requesting new cash advances

Step-by-Step Form Completion Guide

Complete these critical identification fields at the top of the form:

Field Instructions Example

Important: These fields lock after submission and cannot be changed.

Document all expenses from your cash advance with these requirements:

Column Requirement Validation Rules
SIGN Check when verified Mandatory for submission
CODE GL/Account code Must match chart of accounts
DATE Expense date Within advance period
DETAILS Description Minimum 10 characters
AMOUNT ₦ value 2 decimal places
DOC. NO. Receipt number Unique identifier
Best Practices
  • List expenses chronologically
  • Group similar expenses
  • Note currency conversions
  • Highlight project-specific costs
  • Document personal expenses separately

Understanding the Fields:

Field Calculation
CRF TOTAL Sum of all expenses listed
AMOUNT ADVANCED From original CAF
AMOUNT RETURNED Cash being returned
TOTAL Advanced - (Expenses + Returned)
Handling Balances:

Surplus: When TOTAL > 0 (Return funds)

Shortage: When TOTAL < 0 (Provide explanation)

Break-even: When TOTAL = 0 (Ideal scenario)

All variances >5% require written justification.

Acceptable Document Types:
  • Original receipts (preferred)
  • Scanned copies (minimum 300dpi)
  • Vendor invoices
  • Bank payment confirmations
  • Approved expense reports
  • Notarized affidavits (for lost receipts)
Upload Instructions:
  1. Click "Choose File" for each supporting document
  2. Select clear, legible files (PDF, JPG, PNG)
  3. Name files descriptively (e.g. "Fuel-Receipt-20250502.jpg")
  4. Ensure each expense has corresponding documentation
  5. Use multiple uploads for large transactions
Documents must show: Amount, Date, Vendor, Purpose, and Payment method.

Frequently Asked Questions

For lost receipts:

  1. Complete an Affidavit of Loss (available from Finance)
  2. Provide alternative proof (bank statement, witness statement)
  3. Get department head approval
  4. Flag in remarks section

Note: Unsubstantiated expenses may be deducted from salary.

Standard processing times:

  • Verification: 1-2 business days
  • Approval: 1-3 business days
  • Processing: 1 business day after approval

Complex cases or missing documents may extend this timeline.

Form edits after submission require:

  1. Written request to Finance
  2. Approval from original approver
  3. Clear justification for changes
Significant changes may require creating a new CRF.
