Project Management Panels Explained

This section offers a structured overview of key panels used in project execution, administration, and control—designed to ensure clarity and accountability from start to finish.

  1. Project Management

    Initiates the project life cycle by documenting and authorizing the start of work activities:

    • Documents:
      • Project Work Order Form - Authorizes scope, timelines, and stakeholders to commence work.
  2. Project Work Completion

    Used to verify and record that work has been executed as planned and formally closed:

    • Documents:
      • Project Work Completion Forms - Signed documentation that confirms successful project delivery and inspection.
  3. Project Finance

    Handles the inflow and outflow of money, cost records, and financial tracking:

    • Documents:
      • Profit & Loss Statement - Displays revenue, costs, and profits.
      • Cash Advance Forms - Funds released in advance for operations.
      • Petty Cash Vouchers - Records small routine expenses.
      • Cash Retirement Forms - Used to reconcile advanced funds after usage.
      • Cash Claim Forms - Employee claims for reimbursements.
      • Week’d All. Forms - Weekly allocation and fund tracking.
  4. Equipment Monitoring & Management

    Tracks movement, use, and condition of machinery, vehicles, and equipment:

    • Documents:
      • Gen. Equipment Request Slips - Records initial requests for tools and equipment.
      • Equipment Return Slips - Logs returned machinery or tools post-use.
      • Car Rental Requisition Form - Used to rent project vehicles officially.
      • Service Request Forms - Initiates maintenance or repair needs.
      • Waybills - Accompanies transported goods or materials.
  5. Store/Warehouse Inventory

    Ensures efficient stock control, usage records, and materials movement:

    • Documents:
      • Material Request Slips - Request form to draw stock from the store.
      • Good Return Slips - Returns unused or rejected items to inventory.
      • Overseas Procurement Request Slips - Orders for imported materials.
      • Overseas Procurement Delivery Notes - Acknowledges international goods delivery.
  6. Meeting Minutes & HSE Checklist

    Documents discussions, safety preparations, and risk assessment activities:

    • Documents:
      • Pre-Job Briefing Form - Captures task plan and expectations before work starts.
      • Post Job Briefing Form - Debriefs outcomes and incidents after work.
      • Tool Box Meetings - Daily on-site safety talks.
      • HSE PreMob Checklists - Verifies safety before mobilization.
      • HSE HIR Cards - Hazard Identification and Reporting tools.
  7. Checklists & Forms

    Provides structured checklists for ensuring operational readiness:

    • Documents:
      • Work Order Forms - Details activities to be performed under contract.
      • Initialisation Checklist - Validates project initiation requirements.
      • Pre-planning Checklists - Confirms readiness before actual planning.
      • Job Preparation Checklist - Ensures all prep tasks are complete.
      • Pre-Mobilisation Checklist - Verifies site and team readiness before deployment.
  8. Project Checklists Continued

    Follow-up documents to monitor all project execution phases till demobilization:

    • Documents:
      • Job Mobilisation Checklist - Ensures all required resources are onsite.
      • Site Preparation Checklist - Inspects the site for readiness.
      • Job Execution Checklist - Tracks activities during implementation.
      • Job Completion Checklist - Confirms final work is completed.
      • Demob., Close-out Checklist - Final tasks before leaving site or closing project.
